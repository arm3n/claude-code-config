# Tesla Model X — Ownership & Protection Plans (2026-02-26)

## Vehicle Purchased
- **2023 Model X Plaid** — purchased used, Feb 2026
- 22" Turbine wheels, staggered: 265/35R22 front, 285/35R22 rear
- OEM tire: Pirelli Scorpion Zero All Season (T0 Tesla-spec)
- Factory warranty: Basic 4yr/50K, Battery/DU 8yr/150K

## Wheel & Tire Protection Plan (WTPP)
- **$25/mo** (Model X Plaid tier), up to 24 months, cancel anytime
- $25 deductible per replacement; unlimited free repairs
- Covers road hazard damage to BOTH wheels AND tires (unique vs alternatives)
- **Does NOT cover:** curb rash, normal wear, theft, tires <4/32" tread, aftermarket tires/wheels
- Must service at Tesla Service Centers only
- **Enrollment for used:** Schedule "Wheel and Tire Protection" inspection in Tesla app, subscribe within **7 days** of passing
- Inspection checks: OEM tires, >4/32" tread, no curb rash or structural damage
- **CRITICAL gotcha (Gemini-verified):** T&C defines "Covered Part" as Tesla-installed tires only. Replacing a tire at Discount Tire may void wheel coverage for that location. Keep ALL tire service at Tesla.
- Decision report: `~/tesla-wtpp-decision-report-2026-02-26.md` (65+ sources)

### WTPP Cost Context (22" Turbine)
- Single OEM wheel: $900-1,625
- Single tire installed: $400-600
- Full set of 4 tires: $1,600-2,400
- Rear tire lifespan: 15-25K miles (fixed camber accelerates wear)
- Break-even: ~1.3 tire replacements or 1 wheel claim in 24 months

## Prior Shopping Research

## Tesla Does NOT Have Traditional CPO
- No CPO program. Tesla sells "Pre-Owned" with 102-point inspection + short Used Vehicle Limited Warranty (1yr/10K miles after factory expires, only if bought from Tesla directly)
- Factory warranty transfers to new owner regardless of purchase source

## Factory Warranty (Transfers to New Owner)
- Basic Vehicle: 4 years / 50,000 miles
- Battery & Drive Unit: 8 years / 150,000 miles (70% capacity retention)
- Body Rust: 12 years, unlimited miles

## Tesla ESA (Extended Service Agreement)
- **$150/month subscription** ($7,200 over 4 years max)
- $100 deductible per visit
- Adds up to 4 years / 50,000 miles beyond factory (max 8yr/100K total)
- **Exclusionary warranty** — covers all Tesla parts EXCEPT exclusion list
- Must subscribe WHILE still under Basic Vehicle warranty (under 50K miles AND under 4 years)
- **Covers:** FWD motors/sensors/actuators/struts, MCU, HVAC, suspension (not shocks), cameras, electronics
- **Excludes:** HV battery, drive unit, 12V battery, tires, glass, seals/gaskets, weather stripping, shocks, interior trim, light bulbs
- **Cost optimization:** Subscribe 1-2 months before factory warranty expires, not at purchase

## ESA Plaid Coverage — Confirmed
- ESA covers ALL Model X trims including Plaid — no trim-level exclusions in terms
- No pricing difference: $150/mo for all Model X (Plaid and Long Range)
- The old prepaid ESA (pre-June 2025) did NOT cover 2021+ S/X — the new subscription fixed this
- No Plaid-specific exclusions (carbon ceramic brakes are aftermarket, not factory)

## "Drive Unit Excluded" — What It Means
- **Drive Unit = motor + inverter + gearbox + internal components** (all inside the housing)
- Excluded from ESA because covered by separate 8yr/150K Drive Unit warranty ($0 deductible)
- DU warranty OUTLASTS ESA by 50K miles (150K vs 100K) — no powertrain gap
- For Plaid tri-motor: ALL three motors covered under DU warranty
- **Half-shaft gap:** covered by Basic + ESA but NOT by DU warranty — uncovered after ESA ends at 100K (~$2,000/pair)
- DU replacement cost out of warranty: $7,500–$10,900 at Tesla SC; $3,000–$5,000 refurbished at third-party
- Plaid carbon-wrapped rotor motors are newer design with limited failure data so far

## Battery ESA
- Separate product, currently Model 3/Y ONLY — NOT available for Model X (as of Feb 2026)
- $2,000 one-time, adds 2yr/30K miles
- Only option for extended DU coverage on Model X is third-party (XCare, Amber)

## Recommended Third-Party Providers
### XCare EV (Xcelerate Auto) — Best proven track record
- EV specialist, founded by former Tesla employees
- $0/$100/$500 deductible options; up to 10yr/200K miles
- Covers: battery, drive unit, FWD mechanisms, air suspension, MCU
- Rental car included; commercial/rideshare OK; transferable
- Caveat: some Tesla SCs don't direct-bill — may need to pay upfront and get reimbursed

### Amber (AmberCare) — Highest rated, newer
- Tesla-only specialist; Trustpilot 4.5/5, 86% five-star, zero documented denials
- Starting ~$60/month; battery & drive unit coverage INCLUDED
- Former Tesla Service staff; remote diagnostics at sign-up
- Caveat: only ~2 years old, available in ~10 states

## AVOID
- **CarShield** — $10M FTC fine, battery coverage capped at $6K, widespread denials
- **Endurance** — class action pending, not EV-specific, 266-upvote Reddit warning thread

## Owner Sentiment (42 opinions across 40+ sources)
- 33% pro-ESA, 24% pro-third-party (XCare/Amber), 26% self-insure, 17% mixed
- Model X owners lean MORE toward coverage than Model 3/Y owners due to higher repair exposure
- FWD repairs can run $500–$8,000+ out of warranty; air suspension $2,500+

## Common Model X Failures (Most Expensive)
1. FWD sensor malfunctions ($200-$600 each)
2. FWD power strut failure ($400-$800)
3. FWD motor/actuator failure ($400-$800)
4. Full FWD arm + sensor assembly ($3,000-$8,000+)
5. Air suspension ($2,500+)
6. FWD weather stripping/seals (~$2,800) — NOT covered by ANY warranty
7. Window regulators ($250-$600 each)
